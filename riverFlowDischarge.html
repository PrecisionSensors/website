Flow discharge radar system

The flow discharge radar system comprises of two radars. The first radar measures the velocity of the surface river flow and the second radar measures the vertical height of the fixed radar from the river.

The river discharge or volume flow rate in m3/second is calculated from the cross-sectional area of the river together with the river surface velocity and the vertical height of the river surface from the radar.

The surface velocity measuring radar works on the doppler phenomenon whereby an electromagnetic wave (EM) of 24 GHz is transmitted and reflected from the flowing river. Due to doppler effect the reflected EM is at a slightly different frequency which is proportional to the river velocity. The received EM is compared with the transmitted EM and the river surface velocity is calculated.

An illustration of river surface velocity measurement setup from side view, the observation area (marked in yellow) depends on the height of radar and the beam width in elevation, theta (elevation)

The level radar works on 60 GHz and uses Frequency Modulated Continuous Wave (FMCW) concept. It measures the height of the radar from the water surface.

An illustration of level setup with the river flowing into the page

The combined radar system is able to calculate volume flow rate of the river or can be used as an early warning flood radar. During a flood, first the surface velocity of the river increases and then after a period of time the water level in the river starts to rise. By having access to both the surface velocity and surface level (vertical height) data, an early flood warning system can be devised.

An illustration of calculation of river discharge or volume flow rate per second

The flow discharge radar system is comprised of two radar systems: River Surface Velocity Radar and Level Radar. Both radars are available either as seperate units or as a combined unit.
